Relection on climbing 2021-11-19

Posted by Yunhai Blog on Monday, January 1, 0001

I’m more a climber than anything else at the moment. I work for 40 hours per week at my job. But truth is my real working hour is probably just 16 hours. Meanwhile, in a week I do 3-4 sessions of climbing, putting together the cycling, the getting dressed and getting undressed, the after-session chilling, the climbing part of life has a bigger weight compared to my job. But of course time spending is not everything. It is really the passion and energy in there that makes me say: I’m a climber, more than the rest.

In the past two weeks I have been climbing harder relatively again. I’m crimping relentlessly on the yellows and the reds whenever Adrian or Kamiel or Rick was doing those routes. I couldn’t do anything - not a single new yellow, not a single new red. But I’m already over the period when I’m too upset with myself. I’m happy in recent sessions I’m trying to jump on the routes and try a move or two, regardless of if I can do it at all. Crimps, I’m having less fear for them now. it is the often the open hand slopes that hurt my rist badly. I wouldn’t dare to try too much there as every time after trying, there was as frozen 1-2 secs when I couldn’t move my rists at all.

All in all there are not so much new happening in my climbing, even though I’m climbing so often. There are no new techniques gained, no new levels achieved. But some of the climbing buddies among us are leveling up really quick, which really motivates me to train harder. Doris was one of those. Last session when I met her, she did a beautiful green route I couldn’t do before. I only captured the moment when she jumped off, then immediately I was alert of what happened. I chased her and asked her if she did it from start. She brushed it away lightly. Of course she did it from start. And yes, she did it. I came really as a moment of shock. Upon knowing she climbed it, I couldn’t say another word. I merely chalked my hands and hopped on the route. There was a hold where normally I got stuck and fell. but that time I wanted to do it so badly, I did it with fingers matching and switching, all happened in movements without much thinking. And finally, I managed the top. I wanted to leave the scene immediately after doing it, but Doris was shouting at me, “I’m catching up, from now on it is us now to compete.” I feel bitter and pity for myself. She’s got it right. Long ago when I was stuck in my preview stage, which is roughly the moment when I couldn’t do any yellow

Yesterday, notably I was dreaming about one of the red routes. It hasn’t happened to me for a long while, to dream about climbing. I remember it happened so often in the first or second month when I was climbing. It almost felt to me as if the Goddess of climbing passed me a rose after me expressing wanting it so much.
