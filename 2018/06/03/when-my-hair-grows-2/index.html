when_my_hair_grows_2

  |   872 parole   |   5 mins Posted on 2018-06-03 | In novel , when my hair grows

Back in university time, I was living in a dormitory provided by the uni. Crowed with six of us, nothing can be of any private. Each of the dom room was designed with three two layered bed with the size of 90 cm wide, 2 meters long. Two of the beds were placed across each other, and another one aligned with one of the beds along the wall. Mine was the one that’s aligned along the wall, towards the door. Across my bed, there is the tiny studying desk, so small that it’s impossible for six of us to share at the same time. Shortly after arriving this cage, the person who slept on the nearby bed made a deal with me. “Aren’t you bothered by the the noise of door?” “I am.” I replied.”I’m the one closest to the door in the end.” After a few days of shallow sleeping with the constant bang of the door and loud snoring as if made by some mysterious creature in huge figure, I was exhausted both mentally and physically. “By the angle where my head lies, when the door opens in the night, the light from the corridor goes right into my eyes, So I was thinking maybe we switch direction of heads.” “Fair enough.” Previously we were sleeping foot to foot. Ofcourse if he want to change his direction I will have to change mine. After we settled ourselves, he asked me with his voice inches over my pillow, “You sure this is alright for you now? with the light.” “I would rather face the door than leave my back to it.” That was my reponse to him. “By the way what’s your name again?” “Yang.” “Yeah, you are from Tibet, aren’t you?” “Yes I’m the tibetan.” “And you?” “I’m from the south.” He didn’t seem interested in asking more. And then the silence dropped amonth us, the snoring sound raised. But I managed to sleep.

In China, you need to think about the figure of pupulation of this nation all the time. That great number - 1.4 billion, by itslef can explain most of the problems you sense with you life here. Even if you don’t want to remember it, you will be constantly reminded I’m pretty sure. Every morning at 8 o’clock you need to queue for the library before it even open cause there is not enough seats for everybody. People in the queue has to read books quietly and attentively , even during the most bleak days during the winter, cause they are faced with the most voilent competition in the world, of getting into a better university, ultimately of getting a better job opportunity. If including me in the picture, I’m mostly a person standing out of the queue, just walking by observing the people in the queue. I often end up walking into one of the cafe in the university, because I can’t stand the absolute silence and the despire in the library.

Cafes in the university usually have soft cusion, dim light for couples, fancy posters writeen in English with the lastest news of movies that’s only debuted in the rest of the world. Though it can be very expensive for my daily expense, sometimes I can manage to escape the notice of the waiter, other times they insist in following me all the way till I seated myself, and then standing right in front of me unitl I’ve made my order. I woudn’t blame them much for that, for I generallly like the place I went and enjoy the air there. This one specifically is located under ground of one of the dormitory house. With some meal table in the main dinning room, then some pool tables and coffe tables deeper inside. Funny thing is, the resturant call itself “The sunny zone”, whereas there is no change the sunlight can ever get inside. Month ago, there was even a net bar opened 24*7 in the deepest corner. But then some student who can’t manage his own life stayed there staring at the screen for two full days without picking up his phone from his mom. His poor mom has to come all the way from who knows where to the university to pick him up and make a complain to the university so that the net bar is later guranteed to be shut and shut forever.
Me personally feel sorry for the net bar, though I’m never a great fun of games, I enjoy the diversity of people there. That’s what adds the charm to a university. Besides, shortly after the shut down of the place, I wonder almost instantly in the next day, the net bars close by my university must have welcomed a new current of fresh customers. As I said, everything can in to the end be explained by the huge population. Not everyone can survive the pressure of such competition to get into the university, even worse, for those who do surviced for the moment, not everyone can survive the all of sudden relaxed nerves in the university life.
