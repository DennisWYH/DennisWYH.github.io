  |   598 parole   |   4 mins Posted on 2018-06-11 | In novel , when my hair grows

I read the Japanese writer murakami hamuki at the time as I read about many other Japanese writers. He had lots of books both in the library and in bookshops around. Though I don’t have much money for luxury spare, I can easily afford books without being guilty. His work both excited me and confused me, hard to tell which affect weights more. In one of his most famous book, there is a woman character called Green. I liked the part when “I” the main character and Green make love. It started very well at the beginning of the plot, then it became something of great sickness. She was in the end in a mental hospital and they reunited and tried to make love again. But it didn’t happen as she was too dry to proceed. I remember me raising my head off the book to rest upon this plot. I noticed people sitting around me having normal whispearing or buried themselves in studying or just resting after their meal. Everything extremely normal but too normal that it didn’t seem to me a good way of living. That was the moment I felt lonely, that everything around me, everything that’s happening to me was a nonsense to me. So I burried myself again into the book, where there is something true happening. She was to dry to be intercoursed, as if eyes that can’t shed tears.

Those days I kept reading and found myself the only person doing so in the dorm or in the class. I was well aware of the sepearting between me and the real world, the seperating was so close to my eyes that sometimes I felt I could easily touch whatever it is in between us and poke it with my fingers. But then aross the other side there is only bull shits from the lectuers and from the university that I was not wiling to do so. I kept myself away with a distance and only proach it with my own way. Soon after I met a student majored in Japanese and I got the lecture timetable. And I became a regular attendance in their lecture. It was after reading hemingway that I realized whatever books I’ve read in Chinese that was not written originally in Chinese were fake language. And that I couldn’t ever go back reading translated work anymore. Japanese started with being hard with 50 sylebus to remember. But then once mastering them, I can profectly read Japanese articles out loud as long as it’s marked with the souns. I know I can read Japanese well cause I’m from the south of China with a strange dialect that many people used to think I was Japanese whenever I spoke that language. Only that the similarity between Japanese and Chinese bothered me. Later when studying Italian, I came aross with this concept of “false friends” referring to same spelling between Italian and English but with different meaning. With Japanese, the language is almost composed of all “false friends” workds compared with Chinese but with little Japanese words that you can’t find in Chinese. Even worse, those “false friends” are sometimes not “false” enough like Italian and English do, but give you a little illusion of having certain connection to guess from the other language. Students like me alwasy seat in the back so that the teacher could focus on her student who are going to take exam and get credit from it. But I enjoyed it probably more than anyone.
