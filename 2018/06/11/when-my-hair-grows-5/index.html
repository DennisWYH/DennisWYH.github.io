  |   1,544 parole   |   10 mins Posted on 2018-06-11 | In novel , when my hair grows

I read the Japanese writer murakami hamuki at the time as I read about many other Japanese writers. He had lots of books both in the library and in bookshops around. Though I don’t have much money for luxury spare, I can easily afford books without feeling guilty. His work both excited me and confused me, hard to tell which effect weighted more. In one of his most famous book, there is a woman character called Green. I liked the part when “I” the main character and Green make love. It started very well at the beginning of the plot, then it became something of great sickness. She is in the end in a mental hospital and they reunited and tried to make love again. But it didn’t work since she is too dry to proceed. I remember me raising my head off the book to rest upon this plot. I noticed people sitting around me having normal whispearing or buried themselves into studying or just resting on the chair after their meal. Everything extremely normal and even too normal that it didn’t seem to me a meaningful way of staying alive. That was the moment I felt lonely, that everybody around me, everything that’s happening to me was a nonsense to me. So I burried myself again into the book, where there was something true happening. Grean was to dry to be intercoursed, as if eyes that coudn’t shed tears.

Those days I kept reading and found myself the only person doing so in the dorm or in the class. I was well aware of the seperating between me and the real world, the seperating wall was so close to my eyes that sometimes I felt I could easily touch whatever it is and poke it with my fingers. But then aross the other side there was only bull shits from the lectuers and from the university administry that I was not to join willing. Soon after I met a student majored in Japanese and I got the lecture timetable of his. And I became a regular attendance in one of their Japanese lecture. It was after reading hemingway that I realized whatever books I’ve read in Chinese that was not written originally in Chinese were in fake languages. Altered and fiddled with the natural gap between languages and the personal unmatched expereicne of life beteween translators and writers. So I figured since I was reading Japanese writers right now it would be reasonable for me to learn Japanese. Japanese started hard with 50 syllables to remember. But then once mastering them, I could profectly read Japanese articles out loud as long as it’s marked with the sounds. I knew I can read Japanese well like a true Japanese secretly cause I’m from a small and deserted town in the south of China with a strange dialect that many people used to think I was Japanese whenever I spoke that language. Only that the similarity between Japanese and Chinese bothered me. Later when studying Italian, I came aross with this concept of “false friends” referring to words of same spelling between but with different meanings. With Japanese, the language is almost composed of all “false friends” workds compared to Chinese but with little Japanese words that you coundn’t find the similar shape in Chinese. Even worse, those “false friends” are not “false” enough like Italian and English do, and hence give you the illusion of having certain connection in meanings so that you guess from one to another. Students like me mostly sit in the back as if by some order so that the teacher could focus on her true students who were going to take exams and got credit from it. But I enjoyed it anyway and attended it with my full heart.

Meanwhile, lectures from my own school soon proved to be boring. Since it was finance I was studying, everything soon pointed to money and then career in money and then the stories of the lecturers themselves and how they had made their money in this money industry. Students at their young age was fascinated by the grand talk and by the beautiful picture of being successful in their late 20s or possibly earlier. I had no doubt on that. I had no doubt on both how rich those lecturers were, and how successful my classmates can be. The only thing I doubt was if this was what I really wanted to spend my colleage life. Therefore I attended as many lectures I could attend in other fields of study - sociology, phylosophy, politics, history, literature. Materials that I had to read acquired in these lectures soon took over most of my spare time and energy, at the same time, time I spent attending in my own lectures becoming less and less until it reached a minimal amount that I knew I coundn’t go further or I would fail exams. But I knew I enjoyed myself greatly, and that was the only way out for me at the time.

The only place I still constantly visited was “The sunny zone”. Mostly during night, after all my lectures, I would stay there till it close at 2 am, reading materials and writing endless reports. Then I would skip whatever lecture was at the 8 am sesson the next day and went straight for the 10’s. Life like this lasted for a while calmly unitl the taooted girl apeared herself in my life. It all happened too quick that sometimes I doubt if there was something missing that I didn’t notice and that she made up the gap for me well.

It was by no accident that I got to know her at some point because out of many of my optional lectures she was there too. She was also in the shared lectures by my school because we were in the same college. Strange that I had been observing people in my class all the time because I coudn’t find anything better to do under the noise of those professors but I’ve never noticed her. Once while going to one of my favourate lecture in literature, I noticed she was heading to the same building after long walking of the same way. I approached her and eliminated the distance between us.
“Are you heading the the literature lecture now in room 224.”
“I am. And you?”
It was obvious the answer. “I am. How come I’ve never noticed you there.”
“Me neither. How do you like it?” She didn’t slowered her pace at all.
“It’s one of my favourite so far. The professor is really teaching nothing, but somehow this is exactly what I want now. The art of teaching nothing.”
Then I realized she may not like it the same and either she or me would be embarresed by it.
“Sounds like I’ve got a company. I like it a lot as well. it’s the professor himself. He is charming in his style.”
We took neighbouring seats together and waited for the lecture to start. While getting prepared, she lowered herself in her bag to fetch for notebook and pen, it was then I noticed her tatoo under her T shirt on the top of her right bra. From there I could only see part of it as it didn’t show itself the full bounderies on all sides. It really had a weird shape at a glance but I knew I coudn’t stare at it any more so I stayed cool and pretended I didn’t notice anything. The professor who gave this general literature lecture was an poet himself. He wrote folk rhymes and read the latest composed ones out loud in the beginning of his lecture. They were mostly based on some traditional Chinese folk stories he studied and composed upon the same theme with a new language. I seldomly read poems then and now. But I could tell he was a good one judged on the ones he wrote and I liked them much. He gave lectures on topics widely of other famous poets like Rimbaud and Neruda and some other poets, more offen, he talked about his own stories of travellling in his early age, about many times how he risked his life, and about mysterious people he met on his trip. He spoke french and since French is elegant by it’s reputation we all thought he was elegant with the language he spoke. The tatoo girl and I commented on this lecture while he was talking and we enjoyed a lot on that. Then we realized there was more to comment even when the lecture time was over so we took a walk together in the campus to keep talking about the topics the professor raised, on life and death and sex and adventures. The walk later became a style of exploring cogoniton on life like the students in Peripatetic school dated from Greek time. We extended the scope outside of the university and talked more about our own lives, books we’ve read, schools we’ve attend, friends we had and so on.
